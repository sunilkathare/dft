Section 6: Value for money assessment

NPV, including wider economic benefits, expressed in price base year prices. Is derived by submitted ‘net present value cost’ from ‘PVB discounted.’
It divides the New Present Value with each proposal by its CDEL cost to show the returns to the economy of each pound of CDEL. This is the basis on which proposals will be compared across government
Should include all established monetised impacts (For example, journey time savings, operating costs, air quality, indirect tax etc.) and be for the central case. See DfT VfM Framework page 21-22 for details.
Should cover all impacts within the initial BCR and evolving monetised impacts (For example, reliability, static clustering, labour supply); and be for the central case. See DfT VfM Framework page 21-22 for details.
Value for Money (VfM) Category The VfM category is based on the assessment on the assessment of a project’s benefits and costs. In most cases the adjusted BCR or NPV will provide a good indication of the a project. See 5, 6 and 7 in supplementary VfM Framework guidance on categories for more info.
If the VfM category is not aligned with the adjusted BCR and has been uplifted or downgraded following consideration of indicatively monetised or non-monetised impacts, reasons for this should be included here.
